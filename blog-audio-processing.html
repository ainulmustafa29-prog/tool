Understanding Audio Formats

Different audio formats serve different purposes. Understanding their characteristics helps you choose the right format for your needs.

Common Audio Formats

  • MP3: Most popular, good compression, universal compatibility
  • WAV: Uncompressed, high quality, large file size
  • AAC: Better quality than MP3 at same bitrate, Apple standard
  • FLAC: Lossless compression, perfect for archiving
  • OGG: Open source, efficient compression

Extracting Audio from Video

Many tools allow you to extract audio tracks from video files. This is useful for:

  • Creating podcasts from video content
  • Extracting music from videos
  • Separating dialogue from video

Conversion Tips

  1. Use lossless formats (FLAC, WAV) for editing
  2. Convert to compressed formats (MP3, AAC) for distribution
  3. Choose appropriate bitrate based on use case
  4. Consider file size vs quality trade-offs
